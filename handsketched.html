Handsketched plot

Summary

The visualizations created offer a rich, multi-faceted understanding of building violations in Chicago. While the time series plot provides a broad understanding of trends, the heatmap and bar chart offer a detailed view of geographic and administrative patterns. Each visualization type has been carefully selected to address a specific domain question effectively and is backed by detailed write-ups justifying each design choice. Based on the observation of visualizations, we can conclude that the building violations has been decreasing over years. Most of building violation locates from community 30-40, so we can state that the building violation is major in the center of Chicago. Moreover, we can see the violation status is well distributed throughout the community area, all area will have three status allocated in except for community area 80 that have almost open status. In three types of violation status, complied violation status is the most common among statuses, and the open status is the second most common. We observed a small number of no entry status, so it means that the data was well-collected.From the correlation graph, it shows the correlation between inspection status and the categories. For instance if the inspection status Failed is well correlated with inspection category complaint that provides a good reason to inspect the matter deeply since failed complaints will have highest level of negative impact.
